Kangchenjunga

The third highest mountain on the planet

  • Kangchenjunga has an elevation of 8586 m, it is the 3rd tallest mountain on Earth.

  • It lies in the border region between Nepal and Sikkim state of India.

  • Until 1852, Kangchenjunga was assumed to be the highest mountain in the world, but calculations and measurements by the Great Trigonometrical Survey of India in 1849 showed that Mount Everest, known as Peak XV at the time, is actually higher.

  • There are four climbing routes to reach the summit of Kangchenjunga, three of which are in Nepal from the southwest, northwest, and northeast, and one from in India.

  • Kangchenjunga is considered to be a sacred mountain in both Nepal and India.
